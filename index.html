Section 02 · Q26 – Q55

Parallel Axis Theorem

I = Icm + Md² · Shifting the axis of rotation by distance d from the centre of mass

Parallel Axis Theorem (Steiner's Theorem)
The moment of inertia of a body about any axis equals the moment of inertia about a parallel axis through the centre of mass, plus the product of the mass and the square of the distance between the two axes:
I = Icm + Md²
Valid only when one axis passes through the CM. The two axes must be parallel.

Section 03 · Q56 – Q80

Perpendicular Axis Theorem

Iz = Ix + Iy · Valid for planar laminas · Relates out-of-plane to in-plane MOI

Perpendicular Axis Theorem
For a planar lamina lying in the x-y plane, the moment of inertia about the z-axis (perpendicular to the plane) equals the sum of the MOI about any two mutually perpendicular axes in the plane through the same point:
Iz = Ix + Iy
Note: This theorem applies ONLY to 2D laminas (flat, planar objects). It does NOT apply to 3D solids.
